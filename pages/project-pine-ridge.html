Sustainable Housing Unit — Pine Ridge Reservation, South Dakota Completed Summer 2025

Project Overview

The Pine Ridge Reservation in South Dakota faces a severe housing crisis — overcrowding and inadequate housing affect thousands of Oglala Lakota Sioux tribe members, with many families living in conditions that are structurally unsafe, energy-inefficient, and culturally disconnected. Addressing housing insecurity requires both technical engineering solutions and deep respect for community values and identity.

EWB Cornell's Domestic Team partnered with EWB-USA and tribal community members to design a Sustainable Housing Unit (SHU) that is affordable to build and maintain, energy-efficient, and culturally appropriate. The completed unit serves as both a functional home and a demonstration of what community-centered engineering can achieve.

The Challenge

  • Severe housing shortage leads to overcrowding with multiple families sharing single units
  • Existing housing stock is often structurally inadequate and energy-inefficient
  • Extreme weather in South Dakota demands robust insulation and heating solutions
  • Limited local construction materials and supply chains constrain design options
  • Any solution must honor Lakota cultural identity and community self-determination

The Sustainable Housing Unit

The SHU is a four-person home designed around three core principles: affordability, sustainability, and functionality. The unit includes a kitchen, bathroom, and living spaces configured to maximize usable area within a compact footprint. Sustainable materials and construction practices minimize long-term maintenance costs and environmental impact.

📐
Architectural drawings and engineering documentation

Key Design Features

  • High-performance insulation for South Dakota's extreme temperature range (−20°F to 100°F)
  • Passive solar design principles to reduce heating energy consumption
  • Durable, low-maintenance exterior materials suited to the local climate
  • Kitchen, bathroom, and living spaces in a functional four-person layout
  • Comprehensive construction documentation for replication and scale-up
  • Design developed in partnership with tribal leaders and residents

Community Partnership

Throughout the project, the Domestic Team worked in close partnership with EWB-USA and reservation community members to ensure the design reflected Lakota priorities and values. Community engagement sessions informed layout decisions, material choices, and the overall approach — ensuring the SHU is truly a product of the community, not just for it.

Impact & Legacy

The completed SHU provides safe, sustainable housing for a family that had previously been living in overcrowded conditions. Beyond the immediate impact, the comprehensive construction documentation created by the team makes it possible to build additional units using the same design, amplifying the project's long-term reach across the reservation.

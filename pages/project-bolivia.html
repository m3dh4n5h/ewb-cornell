Project Overview

Barrio San Cristobal is a rural community of approximately 300 residents in the Bolivian highlands. For years, families have struggled with access to reliable drinking water and sanitation services, particularly during the dry season when supplies run critically short. The community historically depended on an aging deep well, expensive private water trucks, and rainwater collection — all of which force families to strictly ration water during dry periods.

EWB Cornell's International Team, in partnership with EWB-USA and FIEA Bolivia, is designing and implementing a comprehensive water and sanitation infrastructure system that will provide safe, consistent water access year-round.

The Challenge

  • Outdated deep well infrastructure unable to serve the full community reliably
  • Families forced to ration limited water supplies during the dry season
  • Reliance on expensive private water trucks places financial burden on residents
  • Inadequate sanitation creates health and environmental risks
  • No wastewater management infrastructure in place

Our Solution

The project is designed in two phases. Phase 1 focuses on constructing a sanitary piping network and septic tanks to manage wastewater safely. This will mitigate contamination risks and create a foundation for the long-term water system. Phase 2 will address water distribution infrastructure to ensure every household has reliable, clean drinking water access throughout the year.

Our team works directly with community members and local engineers to ensure the systems are culturally appropriate, affordable to maintain, and built with local capacity in mind.

Community Partnership

In Spring 2025, our team conducted a site assessment trip where we completed over 70 household consultations, surveying water usage, sanitation conditions, and community priorities. This data directly informs our engineering design and ensures the solution is community-driven rather than imposed.

Engineering Approach

  • Sanitary sewer piping network connecting all households
  • Septic tank systems with safe effluent management
  • Community-maintainable design with local materials where possible
  • Water distribution network with storage to buffer seasonal variability
  • Collaboration with FIEA Bolivia for local engineering oversight

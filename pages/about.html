About EWB Cornell

Our Mission

Engineers Without Borders at Cornell applies engineering skills to improve quality of life through community-driven development

Why We Exist

Engineering for Humanity

Engineers Without Borders at Cornell (EWB-Cornell) is a student organization dedicated to developing engineering solutions in partnership with disadvantaged communities. We believe that engineering skills can and should be used to address humanitarian challenges.

Founded at Cornell's College of Engineering, we bring together students from all disciplines — civil, environmental, mechanical, software, and more — to work on real projects that create lasting impact.

What Guides Us

Our Values

🌱

Community Partnership

We work WITH communities, not for them. Every project begins with listening and understanding local needs, knowledge, and priorities.

⚙️

Engineering Excellence

We hold ourselves to the highest technical standards, ensuring our solutions are safe, durable, and appropriate for local conditions.

🌍

Sustainability

Every solution we design considers long-term sustainability — environmental, social, and economic — so our impact endures beyond our involvement.

🎓

Student Development

We develop the next generation of humanitarian engineers through hands-on project experience, mentorship, and professional growth.

Our Story

History & Milestones

2018
Founded

EWB-Cornell was established by a group of passionate engineering students committed to applying their skills to humanitarian challenges.

2019
First International Project

Launched our first international project in Bolivia, partnering with a rural community on water system design.

2021
Tanzania Initiative

Expanded to East Africa with a solar irrigation project in Tanzania, successfully completed in 2023.

2022
Digital Agriculture

Launched our Digital Agriculture team, pioneering the use of UAVs and AI for crop disease detection.

2024
$60K Milestone

Surpassed $60,000 in fundraising, enabling expanded project scope and member travel for site assessments.
